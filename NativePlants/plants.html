Ohlone Native Plant Garden

Mugwort - HÍIŠEN
An upright and aromatic plant. This plant is known for its calming properties and sage-like scent.
Soap Plant - RAWWEN
Found in broadleaf evergreen forests and open scrublands. This plant has many uses for hunting, cleaning, eating, and medicine.
California Sagebrush - MIRYAN
Can often be found in open scrublands and chaparral on the hotter and drier south and west facing slopes of the hills.
Great Valley Phacelia
A soft, hairy annual wildflower, native to the Central Valley and foothills, produces clusters of bright blue bell-shaped flowers
Wild Heliotrope
Sprawling plant with hairy stems and lavender flowers. Used to ease coughing and chest illnesses.
Lacy Phacelia
Fern-like leaves and purple-blue flowers. Improves soil health and soothes skin irritations.
White Sage - Loškowíš
Tall, aromatic shrub with silvery leaves. Burned for ceremony and brewed for colds and indigestion.
Common Selfheal
Purple flower spikes used as general healing for cuts and bruises. Leaves brewed for sore throats.
California Wildrose - Ripin
Bright pink flowers and red rose hips. Used for tea, vitamin C, and basketry.
Yerba Buena - Čarišmin
A minty-scented herb with small white to pale purple flowers and soft, rounded leaves. Found in shady woodlands and coastal forests.
California Hedgenettle - Tawwa
A leafy plant with square stems, serrated leaves, and spikes of small pink to purple flowers. Common in moist meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges
Common Tidy Tips
An annual wildflower with bright yellow petals tipped in white, and a dark center. Grows in open grasslands and spring wildflower field
Red Maids
Fleshy leaves and small, vibrant magenta flowers, this plant thrives in open grasslands and disturbed soils across California
Farewell to Spring
A leafy plant with square stems, serrated leaves, and spikes of small pink to purple flowers. Common in moist meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges
Hayfield Tarweed
A sticky, aromatic annual with yellow daisy-like flowers and slender, resinous stems. Common in dry grasslands and open fields
Chia - PÁTTÍH
A hardy plant with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and purple or blue flower spikes.
Fuchsuaflower Gooseberry
A thorny shrub with maple like leaves, red fuchsia shaped flowers, and spiny, round fruit. Found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
Chaparral Currant
A deciduous shrub with smooth reddish bark, lobed leaves, and drooping pink flower clusters. Blooms in winter across chaparral and coastal scrub. The berries are purple-black in color.
White Clover - Róoreh
A small tree or large shrub with smooth bark, palm-shaped leaves, and large fragrant white flower spikes.
